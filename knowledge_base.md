## Origin and History

Lalitha Sahasranamam is a sacred Hindu text for the worshippers of the Goddess Lalitha Devi — the Divine Mother in her form as Sri Lalitha Tripura Sundari.

### Composition
- The text occurs in the **Brahmanda Purana**, presented as a dialogue between **Hayagriva** (an avatar of Maha Vishnu) and the great sage **Agastya**.
- The Lalitha Sahasranamam was composed by **8 Vak Devis (Vak Devatas)** upon the direct command of Devi Sri Lalitha herself.
- The 8 Vak Devis are: **Vasini, Kameshwari, Aruna, Vimala, Jayinee, Modhinee, Sarveshwari, Koulini**.
- One can worship Lalitha only if she wishes one to do so — the invitation to chant is itself her grace.

### The Poorva Bhagam (Preface Narrative)
The Poorva Bhagam (earlier portion) originates from the Brahmanda Purana and contains a divine conversation between Saint Agasthya and Lord Hayagreeva. Key topics covered:
- The birth of Sri Lalitha Devi
- Her coronation (Pattabhishekam)
- The destruction of the demon Bhandasura
- The significance of the fifteen-syllable mantra (Panchadashakshari)
- The description of Sripuram — Sri Devi's divine abode

Agasthya asked Hayagreeva to teach him the 1000 names of Lalitha, and Hayagreeva taught him this most holy Sahasranamam.

### Unique Distinction
Lalitha Sahasranamam has the unique distinction among all Sahasranamams of **not repeating even a single name** across all 1000 names.

## Dhyanam (Meditation Verses)

The Dhyanam is recited before the 1000 names as a meditative invocation.

### Verse 1
**Om Sinduraruna vigraham tri nayanam manikya mauli sphurat**
**Tara nayaka sekharam smita mukhim apina vakshoruham**
**Panibhyam ali purna ratna chashakam raktotpalam bibrathim**
**Saumyam ratna ghatastha raktha charanam dhyayetparamambikam**

*Meaning:* We bow to Sree Lalithambika whose body glows in vermilion red with three eyes, who wears a crown of rubies with the crescent moon, whose face is full of smiles indicating she is easily accessible. She holds a jewelled cup filled with honey in one hand and a fresh red lotus in the other — symbolising joy and wisdom. Her feet rest on a pot of precious gems, indicating that nothing is difficult for those who surrender to her.

### Verse 2
**Dhyaye padmasanastham vikasita vadanam padma patrayatakshim**
**Hemabham pitavastram kara kalita lasad hema padmam varangim**
**Sarvalankaran yuktam satatamabhayadam bhakta namram bhavanim**
**Srividyam santamurthim sakala suranutham sarva sampat pradatrim**

*Meaning:* Sri Devi Bhavani is to be meditated upon as seated on a lotus, face beaming with joy, eyes soft and elongated like lotus petals, radiating a golden hue, dressed in yellow silk, holding a golden lotus. She is adorned with all ornaments, dispels fear, is compassionate to devotees, and bestows all prosperity. She is the embodiment of Sri Vidya and tranquility, adored by all celestials.

### Verse 3
**Sakumkuma vilepanam alika chumbi kasturikam**
**Samanda hasitekshanam sasara chapa pasankusam**
**Asesha jana mohinim aruna malya bhushambaram**
**Japa kusuma bhasuram japavidhau smaredambikam**

*Meaning:* We meditate on Sree Lalithambika, whose body is anointed with vermilion and musk attracting bees, whose glance itself is a lovely smile, who holds the bow, arrow, noose, and goad — who captivates everyone without exception, adorned with red garlands, shining with the hue of the China rose.

### Verse 4
**Arunam karuna tharangithakshim Drita pasankusha pushpa bhana chapam**
**Animathi bhiravrutham mayookhai**
**Ahamithyeva vibhavaye bhavanim**

*Meaning:* We meditate on Devi Bhavani — light red in colour, whose eyes are full of compassion, who holds the noose, the goad, the bow, and the flowery arrow. She is surrounded on all sides by the eight siddhis (powers): Anima, Mahima, Laghima, Gharima, Ishitwam, Vashitwam, Prapti, and Prakashyam. May I imagine you within me, Sree Devi Lalithambike.

## Prerequisites for Chanting

Lord Hayagreeva explained that for one to be eligible to chant Lalitha Sahasranamam, the devotee must possess:
- **Devotion (Bhakti)** — sincere love and surrender to Sri Matha
- **Purity** — moral integrity in thought, word, and deed
- **Unwavering faith** — belief in Sri Vidya Upasana

## Impact and Benefits of Chanting

### Spiritual & Mental
- Develops the energy of faith to accomplish positive goals
- Generates belief, confidence, and determination
- Purifies the atmosphere and awakens subtle energies
- Acts as a form of penance

### Health & Life
- Provides a long and contented, healthy life
- Builds tremendous self-confidence

### Success & Recognition
- Bestows word power, fame, and goodwill
- Makes one victorious in all good deeds

### Family & Home
- Brings unity and peacefulness to families
- Creates clarity of mind for better performance
- Promotes prosperity
- Ensures no shortage of basic necessities of life

### Practice Guidance
- **Fridays** are particularly auspicious for chanting
- Family members chanting together daily (evening preferred) strengthens bonds
- Can be practised in two forms:
  - **Stotra form**: verses grouped together as poetry
  - **Mantra form**: each individual name prefixed with 'Aum' and suffixed with 'Namah'
